Research Poster Re-Design

Date: Winter 2020

Size: 24" x 8"

Software Used: Adobe InDesign

This poster was designed to update the current research posters inside the engineering buildings at the University of Waterloo. This poster was built using Adobe InDesign.

My supervisor challenged me with this project to create a poster that demonstrates a ‘timeless’ design – and this was the final design. National Geographic and Foundations by Noriko Maeda, a Japanese calligraphy book, inspired this poster.

I began studying Noriko Maeda’s book to get a sense of an idea of how Japanese calligraphy art creates a timeless look. I learned that with Noriko Maeda’s calligraphy, the asymmetry of the calligraphy and the addition of a small bright red signature stamp creates a balanced and timeless effect. I also discovered that the calligraphy Noriko creates looks at sight, language, and time. Furthermore, I also studied National Geographic’s magazine issues to figure out how they created a timeless design that people love and recognize. National Geographic is not only known for its magazine content but as well as their signature yellow border. National Geographic’s yellow border is a great example that demonstrates how simple branding can be tied to a product or message. With these two books in mind, I began to create my version of a timeless design focusing on simplicity, asymmetry but with balance and typography.

During my recent co-op term at the University of Waterloo, I worked on this project from start to finish and led the redesign. I also created a different set of poster designs for this project. These posters have a more modern look that doesn't focus as much on the concept of a 'timeless' design. You can find them down below.

Countdown for StarterHacks 2020

Date: Fall 2019

Software Used: Figma

These graphics were created for the Starterhacks upcoming deadline posts back in December for StarterHacks 2020 applications. Each piece was designed to show the number of days leading to the last day of applying to the StarterHacks 2020 hackathon. The theme of this year's StarterHacks was adventure and travel.

The concept behind these graphics was that I wanted to use our design assets to tell a story that incorporates this year's theme into the designs. The designs I created used symbolism through the use of a flying paper plane. There is a paper plane flying across each design, and in the first post, this is where the plane begins to fly. This first post, with the airplane beginning its flight, represents the start of the final days until applications close. Throughout multiple posts, the plane continues to fly until the last post. When the plane begins to end its flight, it symbolizes the day that applications closed.

The colour choices used were taken from Starter Hacks' brand colours. The colours are vibrant and recognizable to students who have heard or know about StarterHacks.

ARBUS Opportunities Night

Date: Winter 2020

Software Used: Adobe Illustrator

ARBUS Society, in partnership with the Living Learning Community on campus, hosted an ‘ARBUS Opportunities Night’ for first-year Arts and Business students. As a Marketing and Graphics Coordinator, I was in charge of creating the poster for the event, and this was how it turned out. The design is straightforward, yet the message is clear. This poster was shared on the ARBUS Society's Instagram page and was seen by over 400 Instagram followers. Furthermore, this poster was printed and posted all over 6 Waterloo Residences. It was also mass emailed by the first-year Arts and Business advisor to ALL first-year Arts and Business students.
